We start with the end in mind.

This tool streamlines our consulting process in which we work with primary care leaders, practices, and systems to guide them through the PCMH recognition process from start to finish. Our systematic approach defines the scope of the project, analyzes current processes and offers a holistic approach to implementing and reporting the real-time status of the project.

Features include:

  • A guided data collection system that walks you through the NCQA PCMH requirements.
  • Quality reviews conducted by our skilled experts providing feedback through the system with recommendations for you to achieve the best outcome possible.
  • Dashboard displays your status that makes tracking your progress and outcomes easy to see at a glance.
  • Beautiful, intuitive interface that makes the process much less overwhelming!

About DAH Solutions

DAH solutions originated in 2009 to fill a need in our community where the local health collaborative, employers and health plans wanted primary care practices to achieve NCQA-PCMH recognition. That challenge was that primary care practices did not have the staff or the experience to complete the requirements needed to meet the recognition standards and documentation. We rose to meet this challenge by supporting the collaborative, meeting with the practices to assess their current delivery of care compared to the NCQA standards at that time. Using a systematic approach we led each practice through the process, offering tools and support through the transformation of change. The last critical step was to compile the numerous pieces of information/reports into and easy to understand document to submit to NCQA. All practice sites we worked with received the PCMH Recognition-level. At the same time we mentored the leadership within the health care systems to be supportive of the practice and to sustain the process and reporting even after they received the recognition.

Since then DAH Solutions has worked with over 75 unique clients that represent over 300 practices of all types (large and small primary care groups, pediatric and Federally Qualified Health Centers) achieve the highest level possible (99.5% of all practices achieved a level 3 recognition).
